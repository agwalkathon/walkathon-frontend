How it works

Simple. Automatic. Fair.

Participants connect their Strava account once — every walk, run and hike is tracked automatically and appears on the leaderboard within seconds.

1

Register with Strava

Employees visit the registration page and authorize their Strava account using OAuth. Takes 30 seconds.

2

Activity auto-tracked

Strava sends a webhook to our server whenever a participant saves a walk, run or hike. No manual uploads needed.

3

Leaderboard updates live

Activity data is saved to our database and the leaderboard refreshes instantly. Rankings update within 30 seconds of any activity.

4

Admin verifies fairly

Our admin panel flags suspicious activities automatically. Organizers can review, clear or remove flagged entries.

Strava data usage

Minimal data. Clear purpose.

We request only the scopes we need — read and activity:read. Here is exactly what we access and why.

Athlete name

Displayed on leaderboard and admin panel

Activity distance

Primary metric for leaderboard rankings

Activity type

Filter walks, runs, rides and hikes separately

Duration & pace

Pace leaderboard and activity verification

Elevation gain

Elevation leaderboard ranking

Heart rate

Displayed in activity details for participants

Calories

Calories leaderboard and personal stats

Cadence / steps

Steps leaderboard ranking

What we do NOT access: GPS route coordinates, photos, gear, segments, kudos, private messages, followers list, clubs, or any data beyond what is listed above. We never post to Strava on behalf of users.
